You feel cold.


A lot of time passes. Any coherent thoughts are impossible to form. You slowly dream to death.


Your death is suddenly interrupted. Consciousness floods your mind, filling it with alarm. You feel no sensations other than an unfamiliar energy, pulsating through your sentience.


You desperately reach into your memories. There are people, but the faces are blurred, their names unknowable. You had a life before this, but any recollection of it has deteriorated.


With a jolt, your sense of touch comes back to you. Dew covered grass itches your arms and legs as you feel a shirt dampen on your back.


You push yourself off the wet ground, sitting up blindly. A soft patter of rain is audible over the sounds of wind. Cool air pushes against your back, filling your clothes with a scent of the humid atmosphere.


Sight comes back slower, pulsating in and out with the energy in your head. Gradually your surroundings come into view.


A dense gray sky looms overhead, eclipsing the dark wasteland. Patches of yellow grass scatter the mute earth. Clouds of fog drift along the land.


A few meters away from you is an orange backpack, gathering spots from the occasional raindrop. You stand up.
